Thermodynamic Information-Field Model

A computational implementation of stock price cycles based on information thermodynamics

Model Overview

This implementation models stock markets as thermodynamic systems where:

  • Each share acts as an information "charge" creating a market information field
  • Price changes are driven by the field and interpreted as stock-market work
  • Highs and lows are reservoirs of invisible information-energy
  • Price trends convert potential information-energy into kinetic market motion

Model Explanation

Three Universal Laws

  1. Flow: Information-energy moves from concentrated to dispersed states
  2. Equilibrium: Systems seek informational balance
  3. Conservation: Information changes form but is not destroyed

Key Concepts

  • Information Potential (Φt): Function of mispricing, liquidity, volatility, and order book imbalance
  • Field Intensity (Et): Combines order-flow imbalance, volatility, and volume
  • Market Force (Ft): Product of sensitivity and field intensity that drives price changes

Trading Implications

Instead of asking "What news caused this move?", the model suggests:

  • Identify where information is most concentrated
  • Locate steep information gradients where the field can do work
  • Determine the current cycle phase to time entries and exits
